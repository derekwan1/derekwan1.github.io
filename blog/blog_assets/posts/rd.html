Someone once told me that if all the people in the world were to hang their sorrows from a tree, and we could pick anyone else’s, we’d still choose our own.

I don’t remember the context for this, but I also don’t think it matters. I’ve yet to meet someone whose struggles I’d trade for my own, and I don’t think anyone would want mine either. But I’m just a nobody, so that’s not too surprising. What is surprising is this: I heard an interview in which someone with stage 4 breast cancer said she wouldn’t give up her cancer if given the chance. “It’s given me too much,” she said.

To be fair, I think the cancer patient in that interview is an outlier (I think most people would trade cancer for heartbreak or body dysmorphia). In general though, I think the reason people aren't willing to give up their sorrows is that they're tied too closely to things and people we love. I feel that most things people struggle with are indirectly a product of their own values and the people they love. For example, if you struggle in school, it might be because a parent you respect is putting a lot of pressure on you, or maybe you have neurotic tendencies that preclude minor mistakes.
